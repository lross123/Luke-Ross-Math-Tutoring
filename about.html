About Me

My name is Luke and I’ve been passionate about mathematics for as long as I can remember. After achieving an Upper Second Class degree in Mathematics with Economics from Loughborough University and completing an MSc in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Warwick, I realised that I get just as much joy from helping others understand maths as I do from using it in my own research. What began as assisting classmates quickly turned into a rewarding career as a private tutor.

Over the past five years I’ve worked with dozens of students across GCSE, A‑Level and university programmes. Many were struggling with confidence or missed foundational concepts; others needed stretching and challenge to achieve top grades. I pride myself on being adaptable – whether your goal is to pass a resit or to aim for an A*, I will meet you where you are and build a plan to get you where you want to be.

Outside of tutoring I remain active in mathematical biology, applying stochastic processes and data science to better understand tissue growth and complex systems. This work keeps my analytical skills sharp and ensures I stay current with modern mathematical methods that often find their way into my teaching.

Teaching Philosophy

My approach centres on clarity, patience and building confidence. I believe that everyone can succeed at maths with the right guidance and enough practice. To that end, I:

  • Break down complex topics into understandable steps, using simple language and visual aids.
  • Encourage active participation through problem solving rather than passive listening.
  • Provide plenty of exam‑style questions and feedback to reinforce learning.
  • Adapt pacing and content based on how you respond, ensuring you’re challenged but never overwhelmed.

Tutoring Format

Lessons are offered both online and in‑person. Online sessions are conducted via video conferencing with interactive whiteboard software and screen sharing for collaborative working. For local students in the Crowthorne area I’m happy to travel to your home or meet at a mutually convenient location. I also provide digital notes and homework assignments after each session.

Student Outcomes

Students I’ve tutored have gone on to achieve outstanding results, including significant grade improvements at GCSE and A‑Level. Many have described how my lessons helped them gain confidence and enjoy a subject they previously found daunting. While each learner’s journey is unique, my goal is always the same: to help you realise your potential in maths.
